InfiniteCalc

Privacy Policy · Last updated: April 2026

Overview

InfiniteCalc is a local calculator application for Android. It is designed to collect no data about you. Everything the app stores stays on your device.

Data we collect

None. InfiniteCalc does not collect, transmit, store, or share any personal information, usage data, analytics, or telemetry. There are no servers, no accounts, and no network connections made by this application.

What is stored on your device

InfiniteCalc stores the following data locally, exclusively on your device:

Calculation history is saved to a local SQLite database so that prior results are available across sessions. Variables (A–Z, α, β) and custom button configurations are stored in SharedPreferences. Tutorial state (which first-use tips you have seen) is stored in a separate SharedPreferences file. None of this data is ever transmitted or accessible outside the app.

Permissions

InfiniteCalc requests no permissions. The app does not access the internet, your contacts, your files, your camera, your location, or any other device resource beyond its own private storage.

Third-party services

InfiniteCalc does not integrate with any third-party services, advertising networks, analytics platforms, or crash reporting tools. However, as with all apps distributed through Google Play, Google may collect certain device and crash information through Google Play Services in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy. This data collection is outside of InfiniteCalc's control and is not initiated by the app itself.

Users who wish to avoid this entirely can sideload InfiniteCalc by downloading the latest release APK directly from the GitHub repository instead of installing through Google Play.

Open source

InfiniteCalc is fully open source. You can inspect the complete source code at github.com/Zaegan/InfiniteCalc.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, you can open an issue on the GitHub repository linked above.
